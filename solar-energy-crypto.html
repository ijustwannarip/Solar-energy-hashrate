Four reasons this isn't just vibes

Grounded in the documented stages above, not the projected one.

01 // INTERRUPTIBLE LOAD

ASICs cut power in seconds

A mining fleet drops to near zero on a dispatch signal, no equipment damage — exactly what ERCOT's Large Flexible Load program is built around.[2]

02 // PAID, NOT THEORETICAL

Curtailment revenue is real money today

Not hypothetical grid credits — Riot Platforms alone reported $30.6M in curtailment credits in a single quarter.[3]

03 // SMALL BUT REAL SOLAR ROLE

Solar is a minority input, not the majority

Roughly 3.2% of Bitcoin's current mix[1] — a claim that solar already powers most of mining today would be wrong.

04 // TWO REAL TRENDS

Falling costs, independently documented

Solar LCOE and ASIC efficiency are tracked by different orgs for different reasons, both falling for over a decade.[5][6]

⚠ IMPORTANT CAVEAT ⚠

The "frontier" number is the best chip released that year, not what the network runs — real network-wide average was still 31.2 J/TH in late 2024, because old rigs stay in operation for years.[7] This gap is the #1 reason Stage 3 is a projection, not a fact.

◆ THEORY / ROADMAP

What's next — company targets & analyst projections, clearly labeled

Everything in this window is either a company's own announced target or an analyst's forward projection — not a delivered, verified result. Treat it as "where people who build this stuff say it's headed," not as fact.

~5 J/TH[8]
Bitdeer's announced target for its SEALMINER A4 chip — a company claim, not yet verified at scale
10.45 J/TH[9]
Triple-1's Kamikaze III, a real 3nm chip already taped out — shows sub-11 J/TH is achievable today, not just theory
−30% by 2030[10]
IRENA's own projection for further firm solar+storage cost declines, on top of the 90% drop already booked
2nm next[11]
chipmakers' publicly stated process-node roadmap, expected to push efficiency below today's sub-10 J/TH floor
⚠ WHY THIS MATTERS ⚠

If both roadmaps land anywhere close to on-target, the case for solar-paired mining in remote, high-irradiance regions gets stronger every year — but company efficiency targets in this industry have historically slipped, and IRENA's own projection is a range, not a guarantee. Read this window as "the bull case, sourced" — not a prediction.
